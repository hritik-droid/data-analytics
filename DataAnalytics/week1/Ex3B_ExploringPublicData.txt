1. Start by reviewing the data.gov home page. What is this?
Data.gov is a website that provides access to publicly available datasets from the U.S. government. It allows users to search, explore, and download data from various federal, state, and local agencies.

2. Go to Most Viewed Datasets. How many datasets are listed?
There are thousands of datasets available, with hundreds listed under the most viewed section depending on filters and updates.

3. Find the Topic Category for Human Health. Review the range of data sources available for this topic. Why do you think these were tagged with the Human Health category?
These datasets are tagged under Human Health because they relate to health outcomes, medical research, disease tracking, or public health information.

4. Go to the Food Price Outlook. Review the catalog page for this dataset. How can you navigate to additional datasets from the USDA?
You can navigate to additional USDA datasets by clicking on the organization name (USDA) and browsing or searching within their datasets.

5. Go back to the data.gov home page. This time, click on Datasets by Organization.
This section allows users to explore datasets grouped by the government agency that provides them.

6. Try searching for your city, county, and state. What organizations are returned?
Searching returns organizations such as local government departments, state agencies, and federal organizations depending on the location entered.

7. Search for USDA, and click into the result. Within the USDA datasets, search for food composition. Notice that the top three results are all datasets concerning nutrition information. Try searching the USDA page for nutrition. How does that affect your results?
Searching for "nutrition" returns a wider range of datasets related to food composition, dietary data, and health-related nutrition information.

8. Go back to the results for food composition, and click on FoodData Central. Review the catalog page for this data source, then click on the button for Visit Page. Take some time to explore the FoodData Central website.

a) Try searching for butter, or any of your favorite foods. What kind of results do you get? What happens when you click on a result?
Searching for a food returns detailed nutritional information such as calories, fat content, vitamins, and serving sizes. Clicking a result provides a full breakdown of nutrients.

b) Go back to the home page. Find Foundation Foods, and click on Downloads. What kind of data can you find on this page? How big are these files? Where can you find more information on the data types available?
The page includes downloadable datasets containing detailed food composition data. The files can be large (often several MBs or more). More information about data types can be found in the documentation or metadata sections on the website.
